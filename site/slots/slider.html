A slider is a usefull user interface component displaying a list of items or only a part of, when the list is too long to fit on the main dimension in one of the two screen dimensions: vertical or horizontal the page and topics sliders used here are horizontal .

Selecting any of the displayed slides brings it in the front, i.e. focused in the center of the screen the associated zoom effect makes the selected front slide appear in front and outlined . The front slide always triggers a subsequent action when it is acted on for instance by a mouse click or a finger tap .

Sliders have, at their foot, a spot indicator to visually locate the slide position in the full list. Acting on these indicators spots the respective slide on the front. Slides can also be directly scrolled forward or backward for instance left or right .

Page slider

progressiVecontent page slider is a sticky horizontal slider located at the very top of the page. It lists the content components that have been loaded tools components are excluded: skin, bookmark component, etc. , the first one beeing the content graph, which is automacally loaded when first visiting the site.

Page slider indicators have a width in proportion with the number of loaded components: wider for a small number of components than for a high number.

Acting on the front slide or any indicator scrolls the page either upwards or downwards and brings the respective content component in sight. When acting on an indicator, if its associated slide is already the front slide, the slide list is retracted to free up its place for the benefit of the content area, unveiling the progressiVecontent logo.

Topics slider

progressiVecontent topics slider is an inline horizontal slider located inside and at the top of the contents component, under the page slider. It displays the full list of topics defined for the site.
